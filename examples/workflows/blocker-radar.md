# Blocker Radar

Daily scan for everything stuck waiting on someone — including things you're blocking for others — so nothing rots in the gap between people.

Create a workflow that runs every weekday at 9:30am. Scan my recent Slack messages, emails, PR comments, and ticket updates for phrases like "blocked on", "waiting on", "still need", "any update", "pinging again", "bump", "did you get a chance". Two directions: things I'm waiting on from other people, and things other people are waiting on from me. For each, note who, what the request is, when it was first raised, and how long it's been pending. Group by person. Output a short report with two sections — "You're waiting on" and "They're waiting on you" — sorted oldest first. For each item I'm waiting on, draft a soft nudge message I can send with one click (match the tone and channel of the original thread — Slack vs. email vs. PR comment). For each item where I'm the blocker, flag the oldest ones and suggest what I could ship or reply with right now to unblock it. Skip anything already resolved in a later message. Save recurring patterns to memory — e.g. "Sarah tends to go quiet on design reviews, needs a nudge after 2 days" — so the radar gets smarter about who to watch over time.
