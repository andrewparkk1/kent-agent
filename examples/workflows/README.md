# Example Workflows

These are ready-to-use prompts you can copy and paste into Kent chat. Just paste one in, and Kent will create the workflow for you.

Each one is a starting point — tweak the details to fit your life. Change the times, swap out preferences, make them yours.

## The workflows

| Workflow | What it does |
|----------|-------------|
| [Meeting Prep](meeting-prep.md) | Researches who you're meeting and creates private prep notes |
| [Calendar from Messages](calendar-from-messages.md) | Scans messages for plans and deadlines, adds them to your calendar |
| [Weekly Goal Tracker](weekly-goal-tracker.md) | Weekly check-in on your goals and what got in the way |
| [Daily Goal Tracker](daily-goal-tracker.md) | End-of-day review of whether today moved you closer to your goals |
| [Dinner Planner](dinner-planner.md) | Suggests a simple recipe every day based on your taste |
| [Daily Lesson](daily-lesson.md) | Teaches you one concept a day on any topic, building over time |
| [Style Assistant](style-assistant.md) | Learns your taste in clothes and suggests items weekly |

## Add them all at once

If you want to set up everything in one go, paste this into Kent chat:

Create these workflows for me:

1. **Calendar from messages** — runs every 30 minutes. Scan my recent messages from iMessage, Signal, and Gmail for plans, deadlines, reservations, and commitments. Create calendar events or tasks for each one. Include who mentioned it in the description. Don't duplicate things already on my calendar.

2. **Weekly goal tracker** — runs every Sunday at 8pm. Compare my goals to what I actually did this week. For each goal, tell me if I made progress, what moved it forward, and what got in the way. Flag goals with no activity in 2+ weeks. Give me wins and tell me what to focus on Monday.

3. **Daily goal tracker** — runs every day at 9pm. For each active goal, write a short paragraph on what I did (or didn't do) that moved the needle, whether anything blocked me or pulled me off track, and how today fits the bigger picture. If a goal got zero attention, say why. End with one thing I can do tomorrow to make progress on whatever's falling behind.

4. **Dinner planner** — runs every day at 4pm. Suggest a simple dinner recipe. I like Asian food, need protein, vegetables, and a grain. Max 30 minutes, minimal prep. I have an air fryer. Include ingredients, steps, and a YouTube search term. Skip if it looks like I have dinner plans.

5. **Daily lesson** — runs every weekday at 9am. Teach me one concept about [your topic here], starting from basics and building up. Plain English with analogies, then the technical version with real examples. Save each lesson to memory so you know where I left off.

6. **Style assistant** — runs every Saturday at 10am. Search the web for 3-5 specific clothing items based on my style, with links to where I can buy them. Learn from my order confirmations, messages, and browsing. Point out wardrobe gaps. Save my preferences to memory so suggestions improve over time.

7. **Meeting prep** — runs every day at 7am. Check my calendar for the week. For every meeting with a person, search the web for their background and company, then look up past context from my messages, emails, and memories. Create a private calendar event at the same time and date as the meeting with a plain text description of who they are, their background, what we last discussed, and any open follow-ups. Title it "Prep: [name]".
