# Example Workflows

These are ready-to-use prompts you can copy and paste into Kent chat. Just paste one in, and Kent will create the workflow for you.

Each one is a starting point — tweak the details to fit your life. Change the times, swap out preferences, make them yours.

## The workflows

| Workflow | What it does |
|----------|-------------|
| [Meeting Prep](meeting-prep.md) | Researches who you're meeting and creates private prep notes |
| [Calendar from Messages](calendar-from-messages.md) | Scans messages for plans and deadlines, adds them to your calendar |
| [Weekly Goal Tracker](weekly-goal-tracker.md) | Weekly check-in on your goals and what got in the way |
| [Daily Goal Tracker](daily-goal-tracker.md) | End-of-day review of whether today moved you closer to your goals |
| [Dinner Planner](dinner-planner.md) | Suggests a simple recipe every day based on your taste |
| [Daily Lesson](daily-lesson.md) | Teaches you one concept a day on any topic, building over time |
| [Inspiration Feed](inspiration-feed.md) | Surfaces new things to explore based on what you've been into lately |
| [1:1 Prep](one-on-one-prep.md) | Private brief before every recurring 1:1 so you walk in informed |
| [Blocker Radar](blocker-radar.md) | Daily report on who you're waiting on and who's waiting on you |
| [Granola → Followup](granola-followup.md) | Turns Granola meeting notes into a followup email draft |
| [Institutional Memory](institutional-memory.md) | Spots "why did we decide…" questions and drafts answers from your decision log |

## Add them all at once

If you want to set up everything in one go, paste this into Kent chat:

Create these workflows for me:

1. **Calendar from messages** — runs every 30 minutes. Scan my recent messages from iMessage, Signal, and Gmail for plans, deadlines, reservations, and commitments. Create calendar events or tasks for each one. Include who mentioned it in the description. Don't duplicate things already on my calendar.

2. **Weekly goal tracker** — runs every Sunday at 8pm. Compare my goals to what I actually did this week. For each goal, tell me if I made progress, what moved it forward, and what got in the way. Flag goals with no activity in 2+ weeks. Give me wins and tell me what to focus on Monday.

3. **Daily goal tracker** — runs every day at 9pm. For each active goal, write a short paragraph on what I did (or didn't do) that moved the needle, whether anything blocked me or pulled me off track, and how today fits the bigger picture. If a goal got zero attention, say why. End with one thing I can do tomorrow to make progress on whatever's falling behind.

4. **Dinner planner** — runs every day at 4pm. Suggest a simple dinner recipe. I like Asian food, need protein, vegetables, and a grain. Max 30 minutes, minimal prep. I have an air fryer. Include ingredients, steps, and a YouTube search term. Skip if it looks like I have dinner plans.

5. **Daily lesson** — runs every weekday at 9am. Teach me one concept about [your topic here], starting from basics and building up. Plain English with analogies, then the technical version with real examples. Save each lesson to memory so you know where I left off.

6. **Meeting prep** — runs every day at 7am. Check my calendar for the week. For every meeting with a person, search the web for their background and company, then look up past context from my messages, emails, and memories. Create a private calendar event at the same time and date as the meeting with a plain text description of who they are, their background, what we last discussed, and any open follow-ups. Title it "Prep: [name]".

7. **Inspiration feed** — runs every day at 8am. Look at what I've been engaging with — links opened, chats, starred repos, newsletters, searches — and figure out the specific angles I'm drawn to right now. Then surface 5 new things I haven't seen yet that fit those themes: an essay, a repo, a product, a person to follow, a place, a video — whatever fits. One short paragraph each explaining what it is, why I'd like it, and a link. Avoid anything I've already seen. Save new interests to memory so the feed gets sharper over time.

8. **1:1 prep** — runs every day at 7am. Check my calendar for any 1:1s today. For each, compile a short brief: what we discussed last time, what's happened with this person since, open threads with them, feedback I've been meaning to give. Drop it into a private calendar event 30 minutes before the 1:1 titled "Prep: [name]". Skip first-time 1:1s with no history. Save anything new to memory so future briefs get richer.

9. **Blocker radar** — runs every weekday at 9:30am. Scan my Slack, email, PR comments, and tickets for "blocked on", "waiting on", "any update", "pinging again". Two sections: what I'm waiting on (sorted oldest first, with a soft-nudge draft for each) and what others are waiting on me for (with a suggestion for how to unblock each). Skip anything already resolved.

10. **Granola followup** — runs whenever a new Granola meeting transcript appears. Extract decisions, action items (with owner + deadline), and open questions. Cross-reference attendees against memory so owner assignments are accurate. Draft a followup email addressed to the attendees and save it as a Gmail draft — don't send. Save key decisions to memory so they're searchable later. Skip meetings under 5 minutes and 1:1s.

11. **Institutional memory** — runs every Friday at 4pm. Scan Slack and email for "why did we decide X", "does anyone remember…", "what was the reason we went with…" style questions. Cross-reference against my memories, decision log, Granola transcripts, and linked docs. Draft short replies for questions I can confidently answer (as Slack/email drafts, not auto-sent). Flag anything I can't answer as an "institutional gap". Output a weekly summary of questions answered, drafts ready, and gaps worth filling.
