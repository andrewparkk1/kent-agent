# Granola → Followup

Turns every Granola meeting transcript into a clean followup email draft with decisions, action items, and open questions — saved to Gmail drafts so you can review and send.

Create a workflow triggered whenever a new Granola meeting transcript appears (not on a cron — run it when new granola data shows up). For each new transcript, pull the meeting title, attendees, and full notes. Extract three things: (1) decisions made, in short declarative sentences, (2) action items with an owner and a deadline if one was mentioned (if not, ask me to fill in), (3) open questions the group didn't resolve. Cross-reference attendees against my memories and past threads so you know each person's role and what they own — this helps with accurate owner assignment. Draft a followup email addressed to the attendees with: a 2-sentence summary of what the meeting was about, a "Decisions" section, an "Action items" section formatted as `- [ ] Task — Owner (due date)`, and an "Open questions" section. Keep the tone matched to my past emails with these people (formal vs. casual). Save as a Gmail draft — do not send. Also save the key decisions to memory so they're searchable later and the meeting prep workflow can reference them. Skip meetings under 5 minutes or ones that look like 1:1s where a full followup email would feel weird.
