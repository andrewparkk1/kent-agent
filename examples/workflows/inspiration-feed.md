# Inspiration Feed

A personalized "more of what you've been into" feed. Not a summary of what you already read — a curated set of new things to explore next, based on the patterns in your recent activity.

Create a workflow that runs every day at 8am. Look at everything I've engaged with recently — links I opened in Chrome and Safari, articles I messaged about, topics that came up in chats, repos I starred on GitHub, videos I watched, places I searched for, things I read in Gmail newsletters. Figure out the themes I'm actually drawn to right now — not the obvious ones like "tech" or "food", but the specific angles (e.g. "early-stage AI infra startups", "Japanese minimalist interiors", "long-distance trail running gear"). Check my memories for longer-running interests too. Then surface 5 things I probably haven't seen yet but would love: a new essay, a repo, a product, a person to follow, a place to visit, a video — whatever fits the theme. Each item should be one short paragraph explaining what it is, why I'd like it given what I've been into, and a link. Prioritize depth over breadth — I'd rather have 5 great finds aligned with one niche interest than 20 shallow ones across everything. Avoid things I've already seen (cross-check against my recent browser history and messages). Save new interests to memory as they emerge so the feed gets sharper over time.
